HOLLOWFALL is a cinematic psychological survival horror game from Ashen Veil Studios.
Its tagline is "Fear Has A New Home."

Story:
Twenty years after the disappearance of her sister, journalist Aria Vale returns to Hollowfall, a fog-swallowed town the world tried to erase. She finds a place where memory bleeds, the dead still whisper, and every locked door hides a version of herself she does not remember becoming.
HOLLOWFALL is built on dread, silence, unreliable memory, and the terrible weight of what the player already knows.

Characters:
Aria Vale is the journalist. She returns to a town she swore she would never see again.
Father Kaine is the keeper. He knows what happened, but he will not say.
Little Wren is the echo, a child who should not exist and yet does.

Enemies:
The Wailer has a high threat level. It hunts by sound and cannot cross salt.
The Warden has an extreme threat level. It patrols corridors and is blind in bright light.

Features:
HOLLOWFALL includes psychological horror, dynamic AI enemies, four endings, realistic lighting, immersive binaural audio, environmental storytelling, occult puzzles and rituals, stealth and survival gameplay, and photo mode.
Enemies learn player habits and hunt accordingly.
Combat is a last resort. Players must hide, run, and ration supplies.

Game details:
The age rating is 16+.
The campaign length is about 12 hours.
There are four endings.
The game is single-player.
Jump scares are used sparingly. The game focuses on atmospheric dread rather than constant startles.

Roadmap:

Platforms and release:
HOLLOWFALL is coming to PC through Steam first.

The planned launch price is $29.99 USD.
